Tips for Giving Good Feedback on Full Papers

  • Be specific. "The second paragraph of the intro lost me" is more useful than "the intro is confusing."
  • Lead with strengths. Tell the authors what's working before diving into problems.
  • Ask questions rather than prescribe. "What do you mean by X?" helps more than "You should rewrite X."
  • Focus on the big picture first. Is the research question clear? Does the story hold together? Are the results convincing? Save line-editing for later.
  • Check figures and captions. Can you understand the main story from figures alone?
