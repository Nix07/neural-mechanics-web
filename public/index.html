About This Course

Modern AI systems are powerful but opaque: even their creators don't fully understand what the billions of artificial neurons are doing inside. This class teaches methods for probing neural networks to uncover what concepts they've learned and where those concepts are encoded.

Goal: Produce publication-quality research papers studying how LLMs encode concepts from fields outside CS—an area often neglected in interpretability research. Teams will target venues like NeurIPS, ICLR, or ICML.

Teams: Interdisciplinary groups of a domain-expert PhD student, a CS/ML PhD student, and a BauLab interpretability researcher.

Prerequisites

Linear algebra, probability, deep learning, Python, PyTorch. Familiarity with transformers is a plus.
